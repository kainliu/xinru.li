🎉 Welcome!

Wecome to my homepage! I am a PhD candidate in Applied Statistics in the Mathematical Institute at Leiden University.

Currently, my project focuses on integrating tree-based method into the framework of meta-analysis to investigate interactions between moderators. Comparing to traditional meta-analyisis techniques, which employ generalised linear model, the tree-based method has the advantages of dealing with complex interactions, and providing analysis results that can be easily interpreted. My supervisors are Prof.dr.Jacqueline Meulman and dr.Elise Dusseldorp.

Besides this, a few of my research interests are:

  • Tree-based models
  • Recursive partitioning and sequential partitioning
  • Heterogeneity in meta-analysis
  • Moderator analysis
  • Survival analysis
  • Non-markovian multistage model in survival analysis

Used to be a biologist and now working as a statistician, I like working together with researchers from various fields. And I always enjoy applying data analysis approaches to solve broad research problems.
